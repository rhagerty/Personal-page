Hi there! I'm Rebecca.

My early career has been in automotive MfgE until 2019 when I left automotive in pursuit of a more fulfilling career path. I hadn’t found my previous positions challenging and was lacking the passion to return to engineering. I spent a few months traveling and then began working as a personal trainer while I pondered my next transition.

Quarantine brought a swift end to my personal training career, but helped me rediscover the extent of my engineering “brain” as I found myself passing time building household solutions. E.g. I couldn't find a spice cart to fit beside my stove and decided to build my own. I crave complex mental stimulation and began researching career paths that would satisfy this need for mental engagement and variety.

My fascination with technology, computers, and coding began before I can remember. As a preteen I dabbled with html styling for my social media pages (anyone remember LiveJournal or Myspace??) and was an avid video gamer. It didn't take long before I discovered the kismet connection between my technical brain and computer savvy, and made the decision to transition to software engineering.

Enter: Springboard's Software Engineering Bootcamp

I dove head first into the curriculum and was engrossed with the continuous challenges and daily “puzzles” I was solving using the type of logic that I enjoy (math?! algorithms?! Oh yes!). I’m reinvigorated with passion for this newfound career choice, and in my current job search I’m focusing on companies with a strong culture where my passion can be collaborated with other incredibly passionate individuals. Essentially, I’m looking to make an impact and accept a challenge!
